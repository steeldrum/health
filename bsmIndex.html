Collaborative Management of Logistics

Diabetics are required to carefully abide by strict diet restrictions. This includes a diet geared for low-sugar (glucose) content. Some logistics that get in the way to achieve this goal include considerations for other (non-diabetic) family members, meal planning, tracking paraphenilia, etc.

This page is setup to help our Collaborator members overcome some of the logistical hurdles specific with regards to blood sugar level monitoring.

The first logistical hurdle involves getting a test kit. An example test kit is made by One Touch (see http://www.onetouchdiabetes.com).

These test kits are fine for twice-daily glucose level tests. Where the logistical hurdle exists is in properly analyzing the test data and presenting it to a physician.

The Collogistics site provides various aids for handling the above logistics. For our Collaborators' convenience we offer a free iPhone App (access this site from your iPhone: iPhone App for Glucose Tracking). By using this App when you visit your physician you will have more detailed statistics with you provided you bring your iPhone (something you are likely to always carry).

To use the App first establish your Settings. Then on a twice daily basis record the glucose level (result from your test kit). Associated with each level record a proper description of your most recent food intake:

  • abstinent - no food intake for many hours before test
  • stringent - very controlled diabetic diet food intake
  • observent - normal family-shared food intake, but smaller portions
  • indulgent - celebratory food intake that ignored diabetic constraints
  • decadent - regrettable (but delicious) food intake

The App supports truncating old data (such as over thirty days). In addition, as a paid service, the App can provide detailed PDF and/or emailed reports that include a variety of statistics including comparisons to other collaborators with similar demographics.
